Boxious Day + Michael has placed another box

From: Chaucey McLachlan | Date: 2004-09-19 09:26:46 UTC
Michael has placed another box, "Murray's last minute winner". This time he
did it by himself, so that means it is another box I can add to my own list
to go and find. Check Atlasquest for the link.

Today we went to Brooklyn to tackle psychokiwis 2-box EyeSpy Series. We
also had 2 geocaches in the same area to look for. The day was perfect for
a long walk up at Brooklyn because the wind was non-existent and the day
wasn't hot. The Turbine had ground to a halt and was waiting for more wind
to set it going again. We headed off down the road and during our three-ish
hour walk encountered lots of interesting things. Lovely views all the way
along of the south island mountain ranges, the harbour and the straight, the
rimutakas....

We walked past an ostrich farm and there was a wonderful photo opportunity.
I'll see if I can upload a photo somehow. Further along we found the first
letterbox, "post-modern sculpture". We walked past a huge "castle"
(www.castlewellington.co.nz) that we ogled through the wirelink fence.

Heard some Bleats and noticed a little family of goats, complete with little
kids. Michael started joking around and singing "high in the hills there's
a lonely goatherd - yodalady yodalady yodalady hoo" from Sound of Music.
Hehehehe.

Then onto the Radome, a white spherical building that just looks odd! Like
as if aliens landed in it or something. Hidden nearby there was the
geocache "Hawkins Radome" which we found.

Headed on further. We passed a dead ferret on the road, I was surprised to
see it because I forget that we have those in NZ, only seem to remember the
rabbit or possums. (we saw some possums in trees on the way back).Finally
got to the puffball box and stamped in.

Just as we were stamping in I noticed some white flaky stuff...hmm...then I
realised it was snowing! Well, I suppose it is over 400 metres up there on
Hawkins Hill, but I was still surprised. The snow softly fell as we walked
back to the carpark, never getting too voluminous or scary.

Back at the carpark we found the geocache "Brooklyn Blades". I was thrilled
to find a travelbug in the cache (equivalent to a lb hitchhiker). So now I
have to figure out where to release the little guy.

THANKS PSYCHOKIWIS. Michael and I had a wonderful day with heaps of fun
adventures.

Chaucey
