From: Judi Lapsley Miller | Date: 2004-08-29 04:41:12 UTC
A good question! In this case The Playmakers (letterboxers from Canada) have
given the OK for me to link to their clues from my website (where I have the
boxes in NZ grouped geographically). I didn't ask permission to announce it
to the yahoo group b/c it wasn't giving away any information that wasn't
already easily available. In general, it is always good to ask first, just
in case. The worst that can happen is that they say no, which is better than
p*ssing someone off! :-D

I've found that most people are more than happy to have their box mentioned
in the clues. It isn't usually a big deal for a "straight" box, where the
location is quite clearly indicated in the clues. The exceptions are (a)
mystery boxes where giving away the location (even the mere hint of it) is a
big no-no and (b) word-of-mouth boxes, where the owner may not want the box
mentioned at all on the internet. In a couple of cases, I have vaguely
alluded to a nearby mystery box in the clues, but only after asking
permission first.

On a related note, I've started masking the location of all our new boxes -
even the non-mystery ones - to make it harder for non-letterboxers to
stumble onto the clues. I'm hoping this may decrease the chances of boxes
going missing or being vandalized. So I've been giving the very general
region, then masking the location, but then trying to be very specific about
where the box is once you get there. This is to minimise the amount of
bushwacking needed to find the box - ie it isn't good if the clues are so
ambiguous that the finder needs to turn over every rock to find it.

Hope that helps some - and lets hope the hailstorms stop in Wellington so we
can get out and plant some more boxes,

Cheers,

Judi
