From: Judi | Date: 2007-08-10 00:15:31 UTC
The most complete list of NZ boxes is at
http://www.psychokiwi.org/letterboxing/nzboxing.htm
There are also 2 boxes in Napier that I've yet to do the clues for (my
bad!) that were planted a couple of months ago.

As you plan your itinerary, if you go to Atlas Quest
(http://www.atlasquest.com), you can use the trip planner to find
boxes within a certain distance of all of New Zealand's State Highways
(scroll down past the USA routes to get to NZ). It doesn't pick up
mystery boxes though.

I hope you're bringing some boxes with you to plant too! If you don't
want to fill up your luggage with boxes, you can buy plastic boxes in
a variety of sizes here at any supermarket. The best brand seems to be
systema as they have a rubber ring in the lid to make a water-tight seal.

And a word of warning - come prepared for any type of weather as it
can change rapidly. This can be a problem if you're out hiking (even
for the day). Bring good boots and good wet-weather gear (or get some
here). Look for merino-wool and possum-wool clothing in the touristy
shops - it's light and very warm (and you're helping save NZ forests
by creating a market for dead possums). Always make sure you have food
and water - many of the rivers now have the Giardia bug so you can't
drink from them. There is no food in NZ bush - unless you like fern
roots (yuck!). Most of the berries are poisonous. But we don't have
snakes, ticks, or other nasties, so there's one plus for you. Also, if
you bring a compass, make sure it's a "world-wide" compass - the
cheaper ones usually work only in specific parts of the world (due to
how the magnetic fields lie) - the needle does funny things in this
hemisphere!

Happy travels!

Judi
