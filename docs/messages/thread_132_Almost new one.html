From: Judi Lapsley Miller | Date: 2007-03-18 19:26:58 UTC
Hi Catherine - welcome to our group! Asking permission is a good thing to do if you're hiding a box on private land. Public land is trickier. We don't normally ask permission to hide boxes on council land, but we take a lot of care to ensure the box will not disturb sensitive areas (not just in the box location, but the path to take too and from the box).
 
I hope you get up the courage to carve your own stamps :-)
It's quite easy to to do, and you don't have to be that artistic. It's optional to put an inkpad in the box too. We used to always include an inkpad when we were in the USA, but here they're a lot more expensive, so we don't bother unless we think the stamp would look best in a particular colour. You can assume that any letterboxer finding the box would already have a stamp pad with them.
 
I'm really glad to hear that you're planning on placing boxes! Do let us know when you do, and I'll put a link on the website to your clues. We recommend using AtlasQuest (www.atlasquest.com) too - you can either host the clue directly on their website, or use their website to link to your clue if you've hosted it elsewhere. At a guess, over 95% of NZ boxes are logged on AQ, so its a very handy repository.
 
Cheers,
 
Judi
