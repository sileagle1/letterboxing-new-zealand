Commercial letterboxing venture in NZ?

From: Ralph OHara | Date: 2005-08-22 11:45:41 UTC
Hi,

I read about Letterboxing in a magazine article about a year ago. I
thought it had interesting commercial possibilites for some form of
Corporate learning game which involved outdoor activities so I
obtained the domain names www.letterboxing.co.nz and
www.letterboxing.org.nz.

I also have an interest in adventure activities and at that time my
website www.adventurenz.co.nz was up and running but was not
attracting traffic.

I am planning to redesign the adventurenz website initially with a
focus on building a community of people interested in adventure in
New Zealand. I plan to incorporate letterboxing as an activity with
a link to a separate letterboxing website.

One thought I have is that letterboxing.co.nz could be used for a
commercial venture providing letterboxing activities targeted at
corporates and the letterboxing.org.nz site would be non-profit and
focussed on the letterboxing community.

I would be interested in feedback, ideas, suggestions about setting
up the letterboxing site(s) and any comments or interest anyone has
in the commercial possibilities.

regards

Re: Commercial letterboxing venture in NZ?

From: Judi | Date: 2005-08-24 23:59:47 UTC
Dear Ralph - I welcome your interest in letterboxing. Here in New
Zealand it's a fledgling pasttime, but overseas it has been
played for over 150 years. It has never been commercialised, and there
is strong resistance in the international community to do so.

I have a number of comments about your ideas:
1) There is already a non-profit website for letterboxing in NZ. Along
with the AQ website, the Psychokiwi website has coverage of 99% of
publically-available letterbox clues in NZ.
2) Placers hold copyright on their letterbox clues so you would need
to obtain permission from them before using them for commercial
purposes. I suggest placing your own letterboxes.
3) Although I can see how a "treasure-hunt" type game would be fun for
corporate learning games (cooperation and all that...), I don't
see how letterboxing would be a particularly good way of doing that
because of all the additional "baggage" of carving stamps, making
logbooks, and the swapping of stamp images when finding a letterbox.
Letterboxing suits a minority of people who enjoy arty-crafty
activities and the great outdoors. In my experience, the majority of
people find letterboxing a bit geeky. I would think that a
treasure-hunt without the stamping and logbooks would be more suited
to what you're trying to do. Of course this is not "letterboxing"
anymore.
4) An alternative, that might work is to do geo-caching instead (the
same points above still hold re copyright). Or orienteering? Both
pasttimes are far more estabished in NZ, and you don't have to worry
about the arty-crafty side of things.

I get the impression that you have not actually tried letterboxing
yourself. If you did go and find some boxes, I think you'd see what I
mean.

Regards

Judi
