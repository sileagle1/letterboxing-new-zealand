From: Judi Lapsley Miller | Date: 2005-03-01 21:53:51 UTC
Yes we managed a couple of days there in 2003 - not nearly long enough!!
It's such an amazing place - very atmospheric. It is a huge area though
there are plenty of boxes within a few minutes walk from the roads (which
can be amazingly narrow and quaint - with sheep asleep on them in the middle
of the night!). I'd suggest trying to hook up with locals for the longer
walks, b/c the moors can be quite dangerous if you're not familiar with
them. We didn't do "the" letterbox (ie the very first one at Cranmere Pond)
b/c it was too long a walk for me, especially given the short time we were
there. Also, there aren't that many convenient conveniences around the moors
and it is very wide open so no ducking behind a tree. When you're exploring
around the tors for boxes, do beware of the tissues - especially the yellow
ones... (the tors are the only spots where you at least have 180degrees
protection and privacy!).

And trust me - when you get home you'll need to go through the declaration
line at customs - Dartmoor is covered in animal poop as far as the eye can
see. You cannot walk around it - there is just too much of it!!! If you wash
your shoes, hiking poles, etc in disinfectant before you leave it won't be
too much hassle at the airport. You have been warned :-)))

Cheers,

Judi
