One of my boxes (Kaitoke Kayak) is hidden in Rivendell, also known as
Kaitoke Regional Park (about 45 minutes drive from Wellington). It's not a
LotR theme box though. My cousin's husband also runs a tourist venture on
his farm in Matamata where they filmed all the Hobbiton scenes - even the
toilets are shaped like little Hobbit houses. I haven't put a box there, but
there is another one (Bulls Eye) about 15 minutes away in Te Poi. There is
the Lord of the Rings location guidebook, which is a must if you're planning
a LotR-themed vacation. One source is
http://www.whitcoulls.co.nz/featuredbook1.asp?StoreUrl=whitcoulls&bookid=186
9504917&db=nz
Here in Wellington, there is Weta Studios (but I don't know if they do tours
- probably not), and even the airport has a giant-size Golem there to greet
you when you fly in!

The Playmakers from Canada put out 5 boxes in some touristy spots all around
the country. There are so many wonderful places you could visit. The weather
here can be quite changeable so it would be a good idea to have some
flexibility and contingency plans built in.

You know, that would be so neat--to hide a Lord of the Rings series
in New Zealand near the area where the movies were filmed! Really go
all out with it too. Perhaps a series of three (one for each
movie)? Or maybe a stamp with each of the major characters? (Or at
least until you get tired of carving stamps for each character!)

And a clue written in the same style of writing as the book or how
the characters talk. You know, with all that flowery language.

Oooh! Or how about if the clues tell a story--where you're playing
Frodo and have to destroy the ring. And each box is an 'obsticle'
you must overcome to reach the end. And you 'meet' some of the other
characters along the way--some good, some bad.

Yeah! =)

I totally love this idea! I have to admit, however, I'm not really a
big LOTR fan and don't really know much about the story. I don't
really 'get' those fantasy kind of stories, so I'm not really the
best person for 'technical advice' when it comes to LOTR! But
letterboxes--now I know about letterboxes! =) I'd be willing to
help carve stamps and such, but I'd need some direction on what
should be carved. And writing the clue--I couldn't possibily do the
kind of clue I have in mind justice. That flowery writing doesn't
come well to me, but others are *really* good at that sort of thing!

That farm belongs to your cousin's husband?! That is certainly a

Thanks for the link to the book.. it is on it's way to me even as we
speak. I'm sure it will be a huge help in planning out my trip. I
don't feel quite so lost now.

Have you found all the New Zealand boxes?

For Colorado boxes you have many possibilities. I live in western
Colorado (Grand Junction). We are in the Grand Valley...surrounded by
mountains, mesa, rivers, canyons.. and desert! We have hiking,
rafting, camping, fishing, skiing, Indian ruins and writings, the
Continental Divide, wild horses, cowboys... too much to list. One
thing we have in common is sheep, and I imagine sheep dogs. The
national finals of the sheep dog trials is near here.. I was planning
on planting a box there this year. You can find it at
http://www.meekersheepdog.com

I wander all over Colorado, so if you want something planted on the
other side of the state, that's not a problem. We're a fairly young
letterboxing state, so the boxes are few and far between right now
and we'd love to adopt some of your's. You also might think about a
hitchhiker.
