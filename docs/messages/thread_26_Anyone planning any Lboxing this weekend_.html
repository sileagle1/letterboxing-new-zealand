From: Judi Lapsley Miller | Date: 2004-09-23 00:43:43 UTC
I don't think we'll manage to get out this weekend - Saturday we're off to
the Indian Festival in Kilbirnie (the Sri Lankan dosai are just fantastic!).
Sunday we'll be catching up on work missed on Saturday. Monday, if it's
fine, we'll try and get out boxing. We have funny working hours b/c our
clients are in the USA so we end up working in Eastern Standard Time as much
as possible - which also means Monday is our Sunday!

Let us know when you find Michael's box - we want you to be first finder so
are holding off finding it till you do!

My big news is that I've finally broken my carving drought. We went to the
Karori Wildlife Sanctuary art day last weekend and I did some sketches that
I couldn't resist turning into a stamp. I tried out some carving tools a
friend made me and finally got the hang of them. I was able to get really
fine detail much more easily. The tools are made from cannula needles - the
sort used for body piercing (eeeeouch!).

By the way, I wouldn't dream of putting a box in the Karori Sanctuary, but I
might see if I can find a spot outside the perimeter somewhere. I'm not just
concerned for the precious, rare wildlife there, but also b/c of the number
of poison bait stations. That's an issue in a number of spots where we might
also letterbox - I have visions of little kids and dogs inadvertently
getting poisoned while trying to find a box. So it probably pays to scout
the area somewhat to make sure boxes are well away from bait stations. There
usually seems to be notices at the trail heads if there are stations in the
vicinity.

As for spoilers - I'm looking forward to you paying penance :-))) A good
rule of thumb is to not disclose in a public forum any more details about
the box location than there are in the clues. If you get clues by
word-of-mouth rather than off the internet, then best to not mention them at
all on the talk lists.

Cheers,

Judi
