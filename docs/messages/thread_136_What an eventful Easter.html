What an eventful Easter

From: Catherine | Date: 2007-04-21 01:48:48 UTC
Well our intentions were to pick up a campervan in Christchurch, find
Native YEP, head north to Geocache event at Springs Junction and then
return home via Greymouth.

It did not start out well. Getting boys dressed in the morning
youngest had very messy nappy (and the smell). Do we still go? Yes
says husband we have payed for it we have plenty of disposable
nappies we will cope. Did a drive by Travel Bug cache near
Christchurch airport and picked up campervan. Half an hour worth of
instructions (and we still never got the cooker to work) on to
friends place to pack our things into the campervan and leave our car
behind. Must have taken an hour (yes we had too much stuff for the
space available).
No time for any hunts have to get on the road. So close!! Only the
second time we have been to Chch since we learnt about letterboxing
and geocaching and to get so close (only a couple of kilometers.

Headed north occasionaly stopping for a strech of legs and hunt found
some missed some and got to Hanmer Springs were not going to stay
here but changed our minds as everyone was tired. No freedom camping
so luckily got a powered site but only because of a cancelation by
someone else. Got the last spot available. Campervan still not very
orgainised takeaways for dinner as boys very hungry to bed as soon as
it was dark for everyone.

About 3am woken to crying as oldest son (4 years old) had throwen
up. Was not feeling best myself. Boys would not sleep up the top
any more so 4 sleeping in space designed for two adults with car
seats taking up vital foot space. About twenty minutes later I was
outside being sick too.

This sets the scene for our weekend woken every night about the same
time for either someone being sick or dirty nappy. Husband was the
only one to escape. Boys fine during day just sick at night time.

Meet up with everyone just east of Springs Junction. Had a swarm of
sandflys around me all the time. Had candles burning in the camper
van to try and keep them out as best we could. Blew them out and set
of.... It was not long before we heard a crash and there was wax over
a large area! Too late now, picked candle up and continued on our
way.

Add in my trip to Reefton hospital Saturday night because of tooth
ache. After shot in backside and a number of different tablest went
back to camping ground feeling improved. Boys finaly had dinner some
time around 7:30. No I did not eat.

Ended up last night staying not far from Oxford and remember there
was a hybrid letterbox near by. Did I want to find it or wait till I
had found Native YEP first. Oh the delema, not for long as I found I
had left the information sheet at home. So once again very close but
might as well have been miles away as we had no chance of finding it.

Arrived back in Christchurch made the swap of vehicles and was given
a delicous lunch. No I won't have any, oh just a little and peaches
and cream. Home grown peaches freshly cooked just for me last light,
ok just a little. Not a good idea not feeling the best husband can
you drive please.

Dropped husband at work by which stage I was feeling better after a
sleep in the car and could drive. Dinner that night was boiled egg
and slice of toast each for the two boys and I had nothing.

So yes we did have an excellent weekend despite all of the above and
attended our first event and found 12 caches and went to a number of
places we would not have if we hadn't been treasure hunting.

Still having problems with my teeth apointment number two with
dentist on Monday the 30th and still no letterbox finds!!
At least I know with my husbands soccer team traveling to
Christchurch for games just about every other week I have plenty of
opportunity to find it. First game today at home (Ashburton).

Sorry this is sooooo long but it was a very eventfull weekend. One I
don't think we will forget in a while.
