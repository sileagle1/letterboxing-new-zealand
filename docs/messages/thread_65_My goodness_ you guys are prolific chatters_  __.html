About that whole mounting-stamps thread--very few stamps I think
really need a mount on them. Sometimes it's nice, but unless the
stamp is REALLY thin, almost never necessary. Even the thin
Mastercarve (1/4 inch, I think?) holds up pretty well without a mount.

You can see how I've mounted a few stamps in the carving tutorial on
Atlas Quest. In fact, the only stamps I've mounted I did so just to
make that tutorial! Just because I generally don't mount stamps
doesn't mean others won't want to! =) Also has some stamp carving
suggestions for those who are still new to the sport of carving
stamps, though I'm not sure how many of you have access to those nibs
that I think are so wonderful. =)

Oh, and I'm sorry about all the snowmen and stuff on Atlas Quest. =)
It IS getting to be winter here in the northern hemisphere where most
users are located! I haven't figured out how to determine what
hemisphere someone is located in to display seasonally appropriate
themes. Well, I suppose I could ask users to include their hemisphere
of choice, but that seems kind of like overkill....

In other news.... Who's getting excited about the total solar eclipse
hitting your part of the world next April?! It looks like the
totality portion JUST misses New Zealand from the map I'm looking at (
http://sunearth.gsfc.nasa.gov/eclipse/OH/image1/Fig01-HSE2005.GIF ),
but I should think they'll be plenty of boats or planes heading out to
the area of totality to nab a good seat. =)

Amanda and I want to fly to Panama and see totality towards the end of
the eclipse. And hey, it's Panama! Should be an adventure. =) It's
also the closest and most accessible place to be on the totality path
from the United STates.

This eclipse is also an annular eclipse, which I've never seen before.
(For those that don't know what that means, I'll explain: When the
moon is smaller than the sun, it can't *quite* cover the entire
surface of the sun--unlike most total solar eclipses that you always
see pictures of and are important in movies. In an annular
eclipse--even though it IS total--some of the sun still peaks around
the edges of the moon.) Anyhow, from what I understand, annular
eclipses aren't near as spectacular as non-annular ones, but still!
It's pretty darned close and should be quite a show!

So.... Am I the only one here planning ahead for this? =)

Happy trails!
