Hey All - there's been a lot of talk over on AtlasQuest and on the "big list" about double-bagging letterboxes, and the general consensus is that if you're using a good quality box, then it does not need to be bagged on the outside. However, the recommended course of action is to double-bag the contents in the inside of the box.
 
I'm guilty of also bagging the outside of the box, assuming that it helps protect against a poorly sealed box. I'm also guilty of recommending to others that they do this too. But after reading the comments others have made, I agree that it is a bad idea - the bags tend to rip easily and they also take on water and then hold it against the box. People have also suggested that it makes the box look more like trash and that it can make it to easy to spot because the plastic is shiny. The key is to double-bag the contents in the inside instead.
 
Have any of you noticed more problems with boxes that are bagged on the outside? I think we're particularly lucky here b/c the sistema snaplock boxes have a plastic o-ring that should help keep them extra water tight - they seem to be better than the rubbermaid and tupperware ones used in the States. On sale at Spotlight too :-))
