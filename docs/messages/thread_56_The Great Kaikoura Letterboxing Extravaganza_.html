From: epiphany_101 | Date: 2004-11-20 10:44:33 UTC
Well, today my daughter & I met Chaucey & Michael up at Kaikoura (no
doubt Chaucey will tell you of their weekend away once she gets home)
& we went in hunt of the 3 LBs in the area & a geocache.

Little Shag was easy enough to find & made a pleasant beginning to the
day. And after much trekking & wading & being threatened by a grumpy
seal & almost giving up, we were happy to discover Taniwha & What a
Fluke...+ 2 hitchhikers who are now on their way to separate parts of NZ.

Then we discovered there was an easier & faster way of getting round
to the same spot! (which was good as we didn't want to go back past
any angry seals).

Just a note to anyone else who does these in the future (& I hope this
isn't a spoiler) but examine a map of the area very carefully before
heading out if you want to do this in less than 2 hours...otherwise
take a towel (if the tide is in) & watch out for basking seals - they
have a habit of just popping out from nowhere.

Keep smiling,
Amy

From: Judi Lapsley Miller | Date: 2004-11-21 04:10:48 UTC
Hi Amy - sounds like you guys had all sorts of adventures! I'm glad you
found all the boxes and the surprises we left behind. Your advice is very
good, and applies to all box hunting - and that is to never rely just on the
clue sheet. Do check out maps, weather forecasts, etc., to ensure you are
well prepared and know what you're getting into. This is even more true for
mystery-style boxes where specific instructions may be lacking. Even when
the clues sound easy, be on the lookout for changes to the landscape and
tracks. Sometimes a box that is easy in summer maybe impossible in winter.
You can get into trouble too if you've misinterpreted the clues or if the
clues are in error and you end up somewhere else - Linton and I have not
only ended up in the wrong woods but in the wrong county! Oops :-) All adds
to the letterboxing experience...

Cheers,

Judi
